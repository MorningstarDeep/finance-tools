How to Calculate Percentage — All Four Methods

1. Percentage of a number (X% of Y): Multiply Y by X and divide by 100. Example: 18% of ₹50,000 = (50,000 × 18) / 100 = ₹9,000. Used for GST, discount, interest calculation. 2. Percentage change: ((New − Old) / Old) × 100. Example: salary ₹50,000 → ₹60,000 = 20% increase. 3. What % is X of Y: (X / Y) × 100. Example: marks 75 out of 120 = 62.5%. 4. Reverse percentage: (Value / Percentage) × 100. Example: ₹9,000 is 18% of ₹50,000.

Percentage Change — Salary Hike Examples

Salary hike percentage is one of the most-searched percentage calculations in India, especially during appraisal season. If your current salary is ₹45,000 and the new offer is ₹52,000: hike = (7,000 / 45,000) × 100 = 15.56%. If you received a hike of 10% on ₹80,000: new salary = 80,000 + (80,000 × 10/100) = ₹88,000. A competing offer of ₹95,000 represents a hike of (15,000 / 80,000) × 100 = 18.75% — use the % Change tab above to verify any offer instantly.

Reverse Percentage — Extracting Base Price from GST-Inclusive Amount

Reverse percentage is extremely useful when you have a GST-inclusive total and need to find the original base price. If a product costs ₹11,800 inclusive of 18% GST: base price = (11,800 / 118) × 100 = ₹10,000. GST amount = ₹1,800. This is mathematically identical to: base price = inclusive amount / (1 + GST rate/100). The Reverse % tab handles this for any percentage value — not just GST.

Common Percentage Mistakes to Avoid

The most common mistake is confusing "percentage of" with "percentage change." A 50% increase followed by a 50% decrease does NOT bring you back to the original — it leaves you at 75% of the start. Example: ₹1,00,000 +50% = ₹1,50,000; then −50% = ₹75,000 — a net loss of ₹25,000. Another common error: "20% more than X" and "X is 20% more than Y" are different calculations.
