About Finance Tools India

Free, accurate, and fast finance calculators built specifically for Indian users — no login, no data collection, no interruptions.

What is Finance Tools India?

Finance Tools India is a free online platform offering a growing collection of finance calculators designed specifically for the Indian market. Every tool on this site is built to match the real formulas, rates, and financial structures used by Indian banks, the RBI, and the Indian tax system — not generic global calculators adapted for India as an afterthought.

All calculations happen entirely in your browser. No data is sent to any server. No account is required. No personal information is collected. You get instant, accurate results every time.

Why We Built This

Most Indians encounter finance calculators during high-stakes decisions — taking a home loan, planning a SIP investment, estimating in-hand salary from a job offer, or computing GST for a business invoice. These moments require accurate, trustworthy tools that reflect Indian financial reality: RBI-standard EMI formulas, quarterly FD compounding, FOIR-based loan eligibility, and the new and old tax regimes.

Finance Tools India was built to be that resource — completely free, with no paywalls, no ads cluttering the calculator interface, and no requirement to sign up or share any personal data.

Our Tools

We currently offer 10 free calculators covering the most common Indian personal finance needs: EMI Calculator, SIP Calculator, GST Calculator, Salary (CTC to In-Hand) Calculator, Loan Eligibility Calculator, FD Calculator, RD Calculator, Compound Interest Calculator, Simple Interest Calculator, and Percentage Calculator. More tools are being added regularly.

Accuracy & Formula Standards

Every calculator uses the standard formulas recognised by Indian financial institutions. The EMI calculator uses the reducing balance method mandated by the RBI. The FD and RD calculators use quarterly compounding as applied by SBI, HDFC, ICICI, and the Post Office. The salary calculator reflects PF deductions, professional tax, and both the new and old income tax regimes for FY 2024–25. We update our tools when regulations or standard practices change.
