How Do Banks Calculate Loan Eligibility in India?

Indian banks use a metric called FOIR — Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio — to determine how much loan you can get. FOIR is the percentage of your net monthly income that goes toward fixed loan repayments. Most banks in India cap FOIR at 40% to 50% for salaried employees. For example, if your net salary is ₹80,000 and your existing EMIs are ₹10,000, your remaining FOIR capacity at 50% is ₹30,000. This becomes the maximum new EMI you can afford, and the eligible loan amount is back-calculated from this figure using the standard EMI formula.

Loan Eligibility Example by Salary

On a ₹50,000 net monthly salary with no existing EMIs and an 8.75% home loan rate for 20 years: maximum eligible EMI = ₹25,000 (50% FOIR). This translates to an eligible loan of approximately ₹26.3 lakh. On a ₹1,00,000 salary under the same conditions, the eligible loan approximately doubles to ₹52.6 lakh. Adding a co-applicant — a spouse with ₹40,000 income — would combine incomes and push eligibility to roughly ₹73.6 lakh. This is why joint applications are common for home loans in India.

What Factors Affect Loan Eligibility Beyond Salary?

Salary is the primary factor, but banks consider several others. Your CIBIL credit score is critical — a score above 750 typically gets you better rates and faster approval, while a score below 650 may result in rejection. Your employer category matters too — government employees and those at top-tier private companies are treated more favourably. Age affects tenure — a 55-year-old can only get a 5-year loan, reducing eligibility significantly compared to a 30-year-old who can take a 20-year tenure. Existing liabilities, number of dependants, and property type (for home loans) also factor in.

How to Increase Your Loan Eligibility

There are several practical ways to boost eligibility. First, add a co-applicant — combining incomes increases the FOIR base. Second, close or prepay existing loans before applying — every ₹5,000 reduction in existing EMIs adds approximately ₹5 lakh in home loan eligibility at current rates. Third, improve your CIBIL score by clearing credit card dues on time and reducing credit utilisation below 30%. Fourth, choose a longer tenure where possible — a 25-year tenure vs 15 years can increase eligible loan amount by 20–25%. Fifth, compare across banks — FOIR limits vary from 40% at conservative lenders to 60% at some NBFCs.

FOIR Limits Across Major Indian Banks

Different lenders apply different FOIR limits. SBI typically allows 50% FOIR for salaried customers. HDFC Bank and ICICI Bank generally apply 50% for most applicants, with flexibility up to 55% for high-income professionals. Housing finance companies like LIC Housing Finance may consider up to 55–60% for government employees. NBFCs tend to be more flexible on FOIR but may charge higher interest rates. Always check the specific lender's policy — this calculator uses 50% as the standard benchmark.
