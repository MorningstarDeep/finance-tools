What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Instalment — the fixed amount you pay to your bank or NBFC every month to repay a loan. Each EMI has two components: the principal repayment (which reduces your outstanding loan) and the interest charge (charged on the remaining principal). In the early months, a larger portion of your EMI goes toward interest. As the loan matures, more goes toward principal. This is how the reducing balance method works.

EMI Formula Used by Indian Banks

All regulated banks and NBFCs in India use the reducing balance EMI formula:

EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N – 1]

Where P is the principal loan amount, R is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and N is the total number of monthly instalments. This is the standard formula mandated by the RBI for all retail loans.

EMI Calculation Example

For a ₹30,00,000 home loan at 8.75% annual interest for 15 years (180 months): Monthly rate R = 8.75 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.007292. EMI = [30,00,000 × 0.007292 × (1.007292)^180] / [(1.007292)^180 – 1] = approximately ₹29,938 per month. Total payment over 15 years = ₹53,88,840. Total interest paid = ₹23,88,840 — nearly 80% of the original principal. This is why tenure choice matters enormously.

How Loan Tenure Affects Your EMI and Total Cost

Longer tenure means a lower monthly EMI — but significantly more total interest paid. For a ₹20 lakh personal loan at 12% interest: a 3-year tenure gives an EMI of ₹66,430 with total interest of ₹3,91,480. A 5-year tenure lowers EMI to ₹44,489 but total interest rises to ₹6,69,340 — ₹2.8 lakh more. Always try to choose the shortest tenure your monthly budget can comfortably support.

Personal loan rates are higher — typically 10.5% to 24% p.a. depending on your credit score and lender. Use the calculator above with the exact rate offered by your bank for accurate results.

Tips to Reduce Your EMI Burden

First, improve your CIBIL score before applying — a score above 750 typically gets you lower interest rates. Second, make a larger down payment to reduce the principal. Third, choose a longer tenure if cash flow is tight, but prepay when possible. Fourth, compare rates across banks using this calculator — even a 0.5% difference on a ₹50 lakh home loan over 20 years saves you over ₹3 lakhs in total interest.
